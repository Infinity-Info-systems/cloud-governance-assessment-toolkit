# Assessment Methodology

## Purpose

This page explains how the toolkit is used to collect evidence, score maturity, and produce an actionable outcome.

## Method Summary

The methodology is evidence-led, interview-supported, and score-based.
Each assessment moves from scope to evidence capture to scoring to validation to roadmap creation.

## Guiding Rules

- Use the same scoring scale across all domains.
- Record evidence before writing conclusions.
- Distinguish between policy existence and policy execution.
- Treat lack of evidence as a risk signal.
- Prefer repeatable scoring over one-off judgment.
- Separate maturity scoring from risk scoring.

## Assessment Steps

### 1. Prepare

- define the assessment scope
- confirm the domains to be reviewed
- identify stakeholders and approvers
- collect available policies, diagrams, exports, and reports

### 2. Observe

- review questionnaires and checklists
- inspect dashboards, logs, and operational evidence
- compare stated operating practice with documented process
- note contradictions, gaps, and missing artefacts

### 3. Score

- assign a maturity score for each domain
- assign a risk score for material gaps
- assign a confidence score for the quality of evidence
- capture target maturity when available

### 4. Validate

- review the draft results with stakeholders
- confirm any disputed claims
- correct misunderstandings and update evidence links
- lock the final findings set

### 5. Recommend

- convert the findings into remediation actions
- sort actions by risk and dependency
- define a near-term, mid-term, and longer-term plan
- identify quick wins that reduce exposure early

## Evidence Quality Levels

| Level | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Direct | Artifact, system report, or dashboard evidence exists |
| Corroborated | Evidence is supported by more than one source |
| Indirect | The claim is plausible but not fully verified |
| Self-reported | The statement depends mainly on interview input |

## Assessment Outputs

- score summary by domain
- key strengths
- key risks
- evidence appendix
- remediation roadmap

## Method Outcome

The methodology should keep assessments consistent enough to compare over time while still allowing the reviewer to explain context.

## Use

Use this page to keep the assessment consistent across teams and over time.

## Outcome

A clear methodology makes the assessment repeatable and easier to trust.

## Quality Checks

- all major findings should include supporting evidence
- all priorities should be explained in business terms
- no action should appear without an owner
- no report should leave the reader unsure of the next step
