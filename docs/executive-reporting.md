# Executive Reporting

## Purpose

Executive reporting translates assessment findings into a concise view of business risk, operating maturity, and the actions required to improve.

## Report Structure

1. executive summary
2. assessment scope
3. key strengths
4. key risks
5. domain score summary
6. recommended actions
7. roadmap and ownership
8. evidence appendix

## Executive Summary Should Answer

- What did we assess?
- What matters most?
- Where is the greatest risk?
- What can be improved quickly?
- What needs funding or leadership intervention?

## Reporting Rules

- keep the language plain and decision-oriented
- frame findings in terms of business impact
- avoid overloading the reader with raw evidence
- show only the detail needed to justify the recommendation

## Leadership Views

- current maturity by domain
- top five risks
- top five improvement opportunities
- owner accountability status
- 30/60/90 day remediation view

## Metrics To Highlight

- score trend by domain
- number of high-risk findings
- remediation completion rate
- evidence confidence level
- open exceptions or policy waivers

## Good Executive Reporting Looks Like

- short enough to read in one sitting
- specific enough to drive decisions
- traceable enough to stand up in review
- structured enough to reuse across business units
